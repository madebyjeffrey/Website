Constitution

  1. Name

    The name of this organization shall be Student Operated Computing Resources or SOCR.

  2. Object

    The object of SOCR shall be to provide computing resources to the members as defined in 3.1.

  3. Members
    1. Regular Members

      Regular members shall consist of all University of Windsor students registered in any degree program offered by the University and has paid the allotted fee to SOCR.

      1. Status of Membership

        A member is in good standing during a given semester if said member has paid the applicable fee to SOCR for that semester and satisfies any other requirements as set forth in this instrument.

      2. Rights and Privileges of Members

        A member in good standing shall be permitted to use the services provided by SOCR within the limitations described here and in referenced policies.

        Additionally, said member shall be able to request access to any SOCR records that are not classified confidential by the Board.

      3. Membership in the Board

        A regular member in good standing shall be eligible to hold any position on the Board and be eligible to vote in referendums and general elections.

    2. Honourary Members

      An honourary member is a member, who is not subject to the dues of regular members, but is afforded only the rights and privileges as described in 3.1.2.

      1. Conferred

        Upon the signed recommendation of one member, seconded by another member, and a simple majority by roll call by the Board, honourary membership can be conferred upon an individual for a period of no more than two years.

      2. Term

        An honourary member shall retain membership for a period of no more than two years at a time unless renewed in accord with 3.2.3. If this period is between two meetings, it shall be extended to the end of the next meeting.

      3. Renewal

        A current or past Honourary Member may request via the President a renewal of membership, under the same voting terms of the Board as stated in 3.2.1.

      4. Transition

        A member previously known as a member emeritus shall automatically be transitioned to honourary member for a period of one year, after which renewal is required as per 3.2.3.

  4. Officers
    1. Positions

      The officers of SOCR shall be President, Chief Executive Officer, Chief Financial Officer, Chief of Internal Affairs, Chief of Public Relations, Chief of Records, Chief Technical Officer, Reserve Technical Officer, Executive Vice President of Talent Relations, and General members of the Board.

      1. Other Positions

        At the President’s discretion, a Parliamentarian shall be appointed to be the interpreter of parliamentary procedure. The role of the Parliamentarian shall be as described in the parliamentary authority stated in section 11.

      2. Other Names

        The name of the position Executive Vice President of Talent Relations shall also be known and interchangeable with the name Chief of Development. The President may also be known as the President of the Board.

    2. Duties

      These officers shall perform the duties prescribed by these bylaws and by the parliamentary authority adopted by SOCR.

      1. President
        Qualifications
        The President shall be an elected regular member.
        Meetings
        The President shall call meetings, and be the chairperson unless otherwise delegated to another member of the Board.
        Official Representative
         The President shall be the official representative of SOCR, notwithstanding the official duties of other officers or a designee.
        Financial
        The President or the Chief Executive Officer shall co-sign, with the Chief Financial Officer, all SOCR cheques.
        Absent from Duty
        In the event that any Board member is absent and unable to fulfill their duties for a period of one month or greater, the position shall be filled during the absence by an appointee of the President with approval of the Board.
        Agenda
        The President shall set the agenda for meetings at least three days prior to the meeting.
      2. Chief Executive Officer

        The Chief Executive Officer shall assist the President in the performance of the duties of that office.

        In Absence of President
        The Chief Executive Officer shall assume the duties of the President should the President be otherwise unable to carry out said duties.
      3. Chief Technical Officer

        The Chief Technical Officer shall be appointed by the Board and retain the position for the remainder of the current mandate of the Board, continues until a successor is appointed by a new Board after elections.

        Operating Policy
        The Chief Technical Officer shall provide software and hardware support as outlined by the SOCR Operating Policy.
      4. Reserve Technical Officer

        The Reserve Technical Officer shall be appointed by the Chief Technical Officer and shall have all the duties of the Chief Technical Officer when authorized by same or when the said officer is unavailable.

      5. Chief Financial Officer

        The Chief Financial Officer shall be responsible for seeing that all monies of SOCR are deposited to the credit of SOCR in the SOCR bank account.

        Cheques
         The Chief Financial Officer shall pay all bills by cheques which are co-signed by the President and/or the Chief Executive Officer.
        Financial Statements
         The Chief Financial Officer shall maintain proper books of account and submit financial statement changes at the next Board meeting or Annual Meeting.
      6. Chief of Records

        The Chief of Records shall keep accurate minutes of the proceedings of all meetings of the SOCR Board, and make these minutes available within a week of the meeting at which they were taken.

        Public Documentation
        The Chief of Records shall make all non-confidential documents available to all regular members and the Board. The documentation shall be available, up to date, and with revision history where applicable.
        Updating Documentation
        The Chief of Records shall keep all documentation up to date with regards to Board decisions.
        Technical Documentation
        The Chief of Records shall work with the Chief Technical Officer or Reserve Technical Officer to maintain documentation necessary to continue operations.
      7. Chief of Public Relations

        The Chief of Public Relations shall be responsible for maintaining the content of the SOCR website and all SOCR publications.

      8. Chief of Internal Affairs

        The Chief of Internal Affairs shall be responsible for the collection of key decisions made, arbitrating disputes, maintaining documentation, and assuming the role of the Chief of Records in its vacancy.

        Key Decisions
         The Chief of Internal Affairs shall be made aware of all key decisions made by persons responsible and as determined by same.
        Disputes
        The Chief of Internal Affairs shall be responsible for mediating disputes between Board Members and/or members when the dispute cannot be worked out between themselves.
        Dispute Procedure
        A procedure for working out disputes shall be documented by the Chief of Internal Affairs, approved by the board, and published on the primary website.
      9. General Board Positions

        The General Board Positions are members of the Board without Portfolio. A person holding a General Board Position shall not be prevented from holding other positions within SOCR.

      10. Portfolio

        A member of the Board with official duties or title shall be termed to be with Portfolio. A member with official duties or title, also in a General Board Position shall have Portfolio. Portfolio status does not impede voting rights in any way.

  5. Executive Board
    1. Board Composition

      The elected officers, appointed officers, and general board members shall constitute the Executive Board.

    2. Board’s Duties and Powers

      The Executive Board shall have general supervision of the affairs of SOCR between its business meetings, fix the hour and place of meetings, make recommendations to the members, and perform such other duties as are specified in these by-laws.

    3. Meetings
      1. Board Meetings

        The meetings of the Board shall be held on a minimum of every month on a date determined by the President or by a simple majority of the Board.

      2. Annual Meetings

        An annual meeting shall be held during the month of March. It shall be purposed for the reporting of the officers of SOCR to the membership, and any other business that shall arise.

      3. Special Meetings

        Special meetings may be called by the President, or upon the written request of half of the Board. The purpose of the meeting shall be stated in the call. Except in emergencies, at least three days’ notice shall be given.

      4. Open to Members

        All meetings of SOCR shall be open to regular members, but no voting rights will be extended to regular members.

      5. Questions from Members

        At any meeting, the Chairperson shall provide for regular members to be afforded a time to ask relevant questions of Board members.

      6. Quorum

        Quorum shall consist of one half of the members of the Board rounded up. The number of members taken as the total shall be counting the people, not positions.

      7. Lack of Quorum

        If a quorum is not reached, then no decisions can be made that require Board approval. Any other business regarding reports and new motions can be presented, and must be evaluated at the next meeting. A time for a new meeting can be chosen by the members present.

      8. Voting Rights

        At all meetings convened by the Board, members of the Board have voting rights.

      9. One vote per Member

        Where a member holds more than one voting position, that member may only vote once and shall have the same weight as any other vote.

      10. Conflicts of Interest

        If a member of the Board is also a member of another organization with competing interests, that member must abstain from voting where that member or the President determines a conflict of interest. A member of the Board with a conflict of interest shall still count towards quorum.

      11. Breaking of Ties

        In the event of a vote resulting in a tie, the President must resolve the tie. If the President has cast a vote, the President shall retract the vote, otherwise the President may cast a tie breaking vote.

        Conflicts of Interest In the event where the President has a conflict of interest, section 5.3.11 shall be read such that ‘President’ is replaced by ‘Chief Executive Officer’.

  6. Committees
    1. Finance Committee

      A Finance Committee composed of the Chief Financial Officer, a general board member, and another officer shall be appointed by the President after each annual meeting or at any other time deemed necessary by the President or motion by the board.

      1. Purpose of Finance Committee

        The Finance Committee shall aid the Chief Financial Officer in the creation and maintenance of the finances of SOCR.

    2. Program Committee

      The Chief of Programming shall head a standing committee tasked to develop future directions for SOCR. After a meeting, a report shall be presented to the next Board meeting.

    3. Auditing Committee

      An Auditing Committee of three members shall be appointed by the President prior to the Annual Meeting. The duty of the Auditing Committee shall be to audit the Chief Financial Officer’s accounts at the close of the fiscal year and report at the Annual Meeting. It shall not consist of the Chief Financial Officer. At the conclusion the report, the committee shall be dissolved.

    4. Other Committees

      The President may at any time form a committee for a specific purpose. Upon conclusion of the committee, a report shall be presented to the Board with relevant findings. The committee is permitted to be closed with approval of the Board.

    5. President’s Ex-Officio Committee Membership

      The President shall be ex officio a member of all committees.

    6. President’s Official Power at Committee

      In the event a non-required member of a committee is not present at a meeting, the President is empowered to select a replacement. This shall be reported at the next meeting of the Board.

    7. Non-elected Members of Committees

      The President is empowered to appoint one or more non-elected members to a committee where these members have specific expertise that would contribute to the work of the committee.

      1. Elected Members of Committees

        Two or more board members, not including the President, must have membership in any committee formed by the President.

  7. Services Offered

    The services provided by SOCR shall be defined in the SOCR Operating Policy.

    1. Modification

      The SOCR Operating Policy may be modified by motion of the Board or on the recommendation of the Chief Technical Officer, or Reserve Technical Officer when assigned by the Chief Technical Officer.

    2. Limitations

      The services provided are subject to the policies of the University of Windsor and the IT Services department of same.

    3. Liability

      The members may not use the service in a way that creates liability for SOCR, the University of Windsor, or the IT Services department of same.

  8. Disciplinary Action
    1. Service Violations

      While making use of the service, any member who violates SOCR Operating Policy or any other governing document may be subject to disciplinary action by the Board.

      1. Notification and Action

        A Technical Officer is empowered to suspend the service of a member where there exists a good faith belief that a violation is occurring.

      2. Prefer Notification and Explanation

        Alternatively to section 8.1.1, the preferred method of discovery is to query the member where a violation is suspected, and determine appropriate action.

      3. Accusation

        When accused of a violation, the member must be notified at least three days prior to a meeting to discuss the issue. Additionally, the member has a right to defend their use of the service before a closed committee or an open council, or any other provision as specified in the parliamentary authority.

      4. Remedies

        Where the society has incurred monetary liability, the member shall assume these liabilities.

      5. Substantive Violation

        Where the member has been found culpable for a substantive violation, service to that member may be suspended for the rest of the semester in which they paid dues, or two months, whichever is longer.

      6. Repeated Violations

        A member continuing to be subject to disciplinary action more than twice, may have their ability to use the Service removed for a length of time as determined by the Board.

    2. Board Violation
      1. Board Impeachment

        The SOCR Board may, by a three fourths majority, impeach any SOCR Board member for willful misconduct, willful misuse of funds upon empirical proof, willful neglect of his/her Constitutional duties, non-attendance of at least three Board meetings per semester subject to compassionate grounds. The accuser must bring the motion to impeach.

      2. Trial

        A trial shall be run as stated in the parliamentary authority.

      3. Punishments

        A member of the Board convicted, must be removed from that position, and not allowed to run for that position in any election in the next calendar year.

    3. Service and Board Violations Separate

      Any violations described in sections 8.1 or 8.2 shall be dealt with separately and no punishment available in one shall be used in the other.

  9. Resignation

    A Board member may resign from their position by submitting a written notice to the Board at least two weeks in advance.

    1. Filling a Vacant Position

      The President shall be responsible for filling the vacant position, by appointment or election, as the President deems necessary.

    2. Resignation of the President

      In the case of the resignation of the President, the position shall be filled by the Chief Executive Officer until the next election.

  10. Elections

    Elections are to be held annually under the authority of the University of Windsor Students’ Alliance (UWSA) where they are capable of carrying out said elections.

    1. Annual Elections

      An annual election in the Winter semester is to be normally carried out, with offices filled in May.

    2. Elected Positions

      The elected positions of SOCR shall be the President, Chief Executive Officer, Chief Financial Officer, Chief of Public Relations, Chief of Records, Chief of Development, and four general members of the Board.

    3. Vacant Positions

      The President is empowered, subject to approval of the Board, to appoint any positions not filled in an election. These positions are held until the next election. If the vacant positions are from the Annual Elections, if possible the followup election must be in the fall.

    4. Continuity

      The outgoing Board shall coordinate with the new Board to ensure a smooth transition. Appointed positions shall be retained until new appointees are selected.

    5. Review of Parliamentary Procedure

      After any election, the elected members of the Board must attend a session to review Parliamentary Procedure. It is to be lead by an individual knowledgeable in Parliamentary Procedure as appointed by the President.

    6. Call of Election for President

      The President may call an election to replace themselves at any time. If the Chief Executive Officer is also the President, the position of Chief Executive Officer shall be retained after the election.

  11. Parliamentary Authority

    The rules contained in the current edition of Robert’s Rules of Order Newly Revised shall govern SOCR in all cases to which they are applicable and in which they are not inconsistent with these bylaws and any special rules of order SOCR may adopt.

  12. Amendment of Bylaws and Constitution

    These bylaws may be amended at any Board Meeting of SOCR by a simple majority vote, provided the amendment has been submitted in writing to the Board a week prior to the meeting.

  13. Interpretation of Bylaws

    These bylaws are to be interpreted by the Board.

  14. Conflicts with UWSA Policy

    Nothing in this instrument shall be read to contradict UWSA rules regarding clubs. In the event where a contradiction shall occur, the rules of the UWSA shall have precedent.

This document was finalized and accepted by a majority of the board as SOCR’s official constitution on Saturday April 28th, 2012 and amended on August 14th, 2012.
